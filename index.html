MoveOnce · Brand Exploration

Contact sheet — three rounds, eighteen directions.

Aaron, this is an exploration deck — not the final brand. The document is split into three sections. The first is eighteen brand directions across three rounds of thinking, each presented as a cohesive trio of wordmark, mark and texture so you can read each one as a complete identity, not as a mix-and-match of parts. The second is six colour palette studies, all built around your existing primary teal #0D9488, each one paired with a different secondary complement and tested in six real lockup compositions so you can see how the two colours interplay on actual surfaces. The third is six font pairings, all free and served via Google Fonts, each shown alone and combined using text from your voice over script.

The purpose of this document is to stimulate your thinking. From here we need to iterate to a single final direction — one brand world, one palette, one font pairing — and from that I'll build the source-of-truth brand guidelines pack that anchors everything downstream: the website, the video, the deck system, the stationery, the lot.

Have a look through. I'd suggest we jump on a call so I can walk you through my thought process and hear your reactions and impressions on what's in front of you. That's what will help me refine to the final direction.

My first pass. Round 3 · Pin Mark Variations

Third pass. I wanted to push further on the actual map-pin icon, so I locked the modern institutional register from Round 2 and explored six distinct pin angles — each one a different conceptual interpretation of the canonical pin. The wordmark and texture in each direction support its pin angle so you can see the full brand world around each mark.

Direction M

Refined Classic Pin

Sits in the Anthropic warm-minimalism and Stripe Press editorial lineage, with the institutional restraint of AUSTRAC's 2024 refresh. Pin treatment: A bespoke architectural reinterpretation of the iconic teardrop location pin: the same instantly-recognisable silhouette, but redrawn with sharper geometric proportions, considered curve tension between the rounded shoulders and the precise point, and a hollow circular aperture at the centre that reads simultaneously as the canonical pin dot and as a perfect "O". Fully self-contained, no shadow, no embellishment, no map context.

Direction N

Signal Propagation Pin

AUSTRAC 2024 refined RegTech register crossed with BIS Innovation Hub signal-systems iconography. Pin treatment: A precise teardrop map pin rendered as a broadcast source: concentric rings emanate outward from its central point in calibrated geometric intervals, suggesting one trusted update propagating to many endpoints. The pin silhouette remains unambiguous; the rings extend its meaning without decorating it.

Direction O

Aperture Pin

Sits in the Linear and Vercel pure-monochrome minimalism lineage, with the institutional restraint of Stripe Press and the considered geometry of Plaid's brand system. Pin treatment: A teardrop map-pin silhouette defined entirely by its outline, with a large circular aperture cut through its centre so the pin reads as a frame or portal rather than a solid marker. The negative-space aperture implies the address passing through MoveOnce, with a single teal accent ring or arc inside or bordering the opening.

Direction P

Transition Pin Pair

Stripe trust infrastructure lineage with the considered restraint of Mercury and Plaid Institutional, rendered with the soft atmospheric depth of Anthropic's warm minimalism. Pin treatment: Two map pins of identical geometric construction connected by a single precise directional arc — the left pin small, hollow, and rendered in warm neutral grey representing the origin address; the right pin solid, fully filled in teal #0D9488 representing the destination. A clean tapered arrow-arc travels from origin to destination, expressing one-time migration in a single read.

Direction Q

Velocity Pin

Sits in the Mercury and Coinbase Institutional lineage of regulated-fintech mark-making, where a single disciplined symbol carries kinetic intent without losing institutional gravity. Pin treatment: A classic teardrop map pin tilted forward at roughly fifteen degrees, with a precisely engineered horizontal motion trail extending from its base — three or four parallel teal streaks of decreasing opacity tapering into negative space. The pin reads as travelling rightward through a regulated channel, dynamic but architecturally restrained.

Direction R

Coordinate Marker Pin

BIS Innovation Hub digital communications crossed with surveyor benchmark plate language translated into contemporary infrastructure brand identity. Pin treatment: A teardrop map pin silhouette reconstructed as a precision survey benchmark: fine crosshair through the centre dot, small tick marks at the four cardinal points around the pin's circular head, the proportions of a geospatial coordinate marker. Reads as authoritative geospatial record-keeping, vector-crisp and contemporary, not vintage.

Six font pairings

Six font pairings for you. All free, all served via Google Fonts — no licensing complications, no foundry fees, instant load on every device. Each pairing has a Primary font for titles and section headers and a Secondary font for body copy. I've shown each on its own first, then combined as title plus body using text from your voice over script — so you're reading the typography in your own brand voice, not in lorem ipsum.

Inter / Inter

Inter is the default of contemporary tech infrastructure. Vercel, Linear, Notion, every modern SaaS at scale uses it. Highly legible, broad weight range, free everywhere. The safest of the six — but safe because it's everywhere, which means it lands as competent and contemporary without giving the brand anything visually distinctive.

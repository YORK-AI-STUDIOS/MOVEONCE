Section 04

The logo mark — three options.

The mark uses the same square vessel and negative-space house as the wordmark’s O, with a location pin at its centre. One update, one place, one home. Three pin treatments stand for selection. The hero treatment is teal vessel, dark pin, white ground.

Mark I · Pin Baseline

Full pin head, dot counter, flat-cut tip. Place and home in one glyph.

Mark II · Pin Squared Head

Pin rebuilt in brand geometry — superellipse head, square dot.

Mark III · Chimney Cap

L1 pin in a house with a small chimney and capstone detail.

Lockup · Mark beside wordmark

Horizontal lockup for headers, documents and signatures. Mark or wordmark can also stand alone.

Section 05

The Network Globe.

The texture is the product drawn as atmosphere. A wireframe globe rises from below the frame. House-shaped nodes dot its surface — each one a home, each one with its own quiet teal glow. Two hub vessels each carry four or five great-circle arcs to their neighbours; every other node connects in. Nothing floats. The hub is the home; the spokes are the organisations.

Section 07

What we lock in to ship.

From here we narrow to a single direction across three calls, and we build the source-of-truth brand guidelines pack that anchors everything downstream — website, film, deck system, stationery, the lot.

  1. Wordmark cut. Base, Heavy, or Signature O. Our recommendation: Heavy.
  2. Logo mark. Mark I baseline, Mark II squared head, or Mark III chimney cap.
  3. Palette. Federal Authority or Onyx Precision. We can run both as parallel systems if useful (consumer vs product), but a primary needs to lead.

Once those three are locked, brand guidelines pack delivered within a week. The film script (V1.1 AV, ~4:00) is sitting in your Gmail awaiting your distillation call separately.
